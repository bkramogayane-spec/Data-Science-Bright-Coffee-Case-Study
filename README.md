# ☕ Bright Coffee Shop – Sales Analysis Case Study

## 📌 Project Overview
Bright Coffee Shop is a growing retail coffee business operating across multiple store locations, including Lower Manhattan, Hell’s Kitchen, and Astoria. In 2026, a newly appointed CEO required data‑driven insights to support strategic decision‑making around revenue growth and product performance.

This project analyses historical transactional sales data to transform raw data into actionable business insights using SQL, analytics, and dashboard visualisation.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives
The key objectives of this project are to:
- Identify top revenue‑generating products and categories
- Analyse sales trends by time of day
- Compare store‑level performance
- Understand customer spending behaviour using Average Transaction Value (ATV)
- Provide practical recommendations to increase revenue

---
## 🗂️ Dataset Description
The dataset contains daily transactional sales records, capturing customer purchases at a granular level.
Key attributes include:

-Transaction date and time
- Quantity sold
- Unit price
- Product category, type, and detail
- Store location

---
## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- SQL (DataBricks) – Data cleaning, transformation, and analysis
- GoogleLooker – Dashboard design and performance visualisation
- Microsoft Excel – Exploratory analysis and validation
- Miro – Project planning and timeline (Gantt chart) creation
- PowerPoint – Executive‑level reporting
----
## 📊 Analysis Performed

Data cleaning and preparation (price formatting, revenue calculation)
- Revenue analysis by: Product category, Individual products, Store location, Time of day
- Daily transaction trend analysis
- Average Transaction Value (ATV) analysis
- Identification of best‑selling products
- Store and time‑based performance comparisons

---
## 🔍 Key Insights

- Coffee and Tea dominate overall revenue, driven by a small number of high‑performing products such as Barista Espresso and Brewed Chai Tea.
- Morning periods show the highest sales volumes across all locations, reflecting commuter demand.
- Revenue growth is largely driven by increasing transaction volume rather than higher spend per transaction.
- Hell’s Kitchen achieves higher average spend per transaction, while Lower Manhattan drives revenue through high customer volume.
- Daily transaction volumes show a strong upward trend, indicating sustained business growth.

----
## 💡 Recommendations

- Introduce product bundles (e.g., coffee + bakery item) to increase Average Transaction Value
- Focus upselling efforts during morning peak hours
- Implement time‑based promotions to boost afternoon and evening sales
- Leverage best‑selling products to drive cross‑selling
- Apply location‑specific strategies based on customer behaviour
- Strengthen bakery sales as an add‑on category
